History:

It was in the middle of 11th grade where I discovered my interest in programming. After learning about functions, I built a 600-700 LoC text-based game (with MCQ’s) which involved a ton of functions. You landed in a room and had to go out. But to go out, you needed a key and …. the game just went on. Soon after, I discovered the beautiful <graphics.h> (I was using Turbo C++ [shudders]). By mid 12th grade I had recreated many games in C++ (Galaga, Brick Breaker, A Who Wants to be a Millionaire, A bouncy ball varied with gravity and energy, and the dino game from Google Chrome using a unicyclist and bullets). I started making a text-based game creating language using linked lists, however I was unable to finish that due to my exams.
